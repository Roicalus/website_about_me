About Me

Hi! My name is Shahzod. I’m interested in technology,
learning, and creating ideas,
always seeking growth while balancing logic with reflection.


I find inspiration in nature:
its quiet, fresh air, solitary walks among trees, rain, and peaceful landscapes.
In these moments, I feel harmony, clarity of thought, and true freedom.

My favorite movies

“The Immortal” isn’t just a film; it’s a stark
reminder of the fragility of human connections.
It shows how easily fate can erase what we’ve
grown attached to, and how quickly the most
meaningful moments disappear — as if they never
belonged to us at all.

After watching it, I was left in a strange state,
where it’s hard to tell sadness from light or
despair. I never expected a story could affect me
so deeply. You get used to people, to their
presence, to their meaning — and then suddenly,
it’s all gone. And even though it’s just a script, it
reflects life too accurately: everything valuable is
fleeting and mortal. Perhaps that’s exactly what
drives us to keep moving forward.
