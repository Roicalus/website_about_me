Favorite Anime

To Your Eternity

"To your eternity" isn't just a film; it's a stark reminder of the fragility of human connections. It shows how easily fate can erase what we've grown attached to, and how quickly the most meaningful moments disappear.

After watching it, I was left in a strange state, where it's hard to tell sadness from light or despair. You get used to people, to their presence, to their meaning — and then suddenly, it's all gone.

Dr. Stone

"Dr. Stone" isn't just an anime; it's a reflection on human ingenuity and the power of curiosity. It shows how knowledge and persistence can rebuild what was lost.

After watching it, I felt a mix of awe and hope. The story reminds you that progress is fragile, yet unstoppable. Perhaps that's the lesson: even when everything seems frozen or impossible, our drive to create, learn, and survive keeps us alive.

Frieren: Beyond Journey's End

"Frieren: Beyond Journey's End" isn't just an anime; it's a meditation on time, memory, and the fleeting nature of life. It follows Frieren, an immortal elf, reflecting on the lives she has touched.

After watching it, I felt a quiet melancholy. Life is meaningful not by its length, but by the moments we notice, the people we care for, and the memories we carry forward.

Dandadan

"Dandadan" isn't just an anime; it's a whirlwind meditation on belief, power, and the chaotic beauty of the supernatural. It follows Momo Ayase, who believes in ghosts but denies aliens, and Ken "Okarun" Takakura, who believes in aliens but can't stand spirits.

After watching it, I felt exhilaration and wonder. The story shows that the world is stranger than we think, full of spirits, aliens, and unexpected allies.
